My first week of coding was a mix of excitement and confusion. I had heard that learning to code could be hard, but I did not fully understand how hard it would be until I started trying to build things myself.

At first, everything felt new. The words, the symbols, and the way code was organized looked strange to me. I had to slow down and take things one step at a time. I learned that making mistakes is a normal part of the process, and that each mistake teaches me something.

One thing that helped me the most was breaking big problems into smaller pieces. Instead of trying to do everything at once, I focused on one small task and solved it step by step. That made the work feel much less scary.

I also learned that coding is not just about typing commands. It is about being patient, thinking carefully, and being willing to try again. Even when something did not work the first time, I kept going and learned from it.

By the end of the week, I felt proud of myself. I had not mastered everything, but I had started building real skills. I am excited to keep learning and to see how much more I can do next week.
